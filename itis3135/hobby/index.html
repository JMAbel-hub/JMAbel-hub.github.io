Hobby Site Midterm

Overview:

For this assignment I've decided to make a page where I review some of my current favorite video games where each section represents a different game review with a total of 6 sections allowing 5 game reviews total on the page. Each review will include two paragraphs where I will describe the game in detail as well as include my thoughts and opinions which should justify my time spent with the game as well as my rating.

I've decided to include some recent games as well as some of my all time favorites in this collection to cover a wider variety of games so you can hopefully find something that peaks your interest. The games I've decided to review are as follows: Elden Ring, League of Legends, Overwatch, Counter Strike: Global Offensive, and Cuphead. All of these games I can say I have thoroughly enjoyed and most likely still play occasionally.

Elden Ring:

The most recent game released on this list, Elden Ring is a third person action role-playing game developed by FromSoftware Inc. The game has a heavy focus on combat and exploration where the player takes the role of a lowly tarnished adventuring in pursuit of becoming the coveted Elden Lord. This game is very unique in that it does not lock any content behind any walls and the entire world can be explored without progressing the story allowing curiousity to reign supreme.

At the time of this writing, I have yet to complete the entire game, however I've played enough to with out a doubt, reccomend this game to anyone. The game lets you approach it at your own rate and everything in it feels completely unique aided by the beautiful environments. For more casual players the game might be a little too difficult, however the combat and exploratory aspects make this game simply impossible to pass up on. My only gripe with this game is that some assets are reused a little too much, with some bosses reoccuring a little too often later in the game.

League of Legends:

By far the most popular game on this list, League of Legends is a mulitplayer online battle arena video game that was developed by Riot Games. The game purely serves as a competitive multiplayer game where 10 players are put against eachother in a 5v5 format where they compete to see which side functions better as a team and generally outplay their opponents. The unique part of this game is the 140 and counting playble characters (commonly known as champions) all with 5 unique abilities and passives for them to use against their opponents.

Although I have by far the most hours in this game out of any of the games I've played, it is very difficult to actully reccomend this game. It is an incredibly difficult game to understand to the point where I would say that it would take a year of consistently playing to actually comprehend most of the things in the game to an satisfactory level. I've been playing the game for upwards of 10 years and I'm still learning new things in the game. The game is everchanging, so there is always a new strategy to the game which keeps the game interesting, but also makes it incredibly difficult to grasp for newer players.

Overwatch:

Overwatch is a class-based online mulitplayer shooter that puts 12 players in a 6v6 game where they compete to complete an objective. In this scenario one side plays attack and the other plays defense where the attackers are actively trying to complete an objects while the defenders are trying to stop them. Each team is able to select characters to play from a roster of 32 playable characters (known as heros) with unique abilities as well as an assigned role (tank, damage, support) which a team can only have up to 2 of making a team of 6.

After playing this game extensively this game is pretty easy to reccomend to anyone interested in shooter games. Overwatch has a very high emphasis on teamwork with it being required to play it at its highest level, however it is not necessary enough to warrant any complaints. The game is very flexible in that it can be as casual or as competitive as the player wants with plenty of fun gamemodes and new things to try as well as a ranked gamemode to test a players skill.

Counter Strike: Global Offesnive:

Counter-Strike is an objective-based, multiplayer first-person shooter. Two opposing teams of 5: the Terrorists and the Counter Terrorists compete in game modes to complete objectives, such as securing a location to plant or defuse a bomb and rescuing or guarding hostages where players play rounds in a best of 30 format. After each round played in the game, the player is given money depending on how their team did and if they completed any objective where they can then participate in the unique part of this game, the economy system. Each player has to allocate their money in order to buy guns, armor, and grenades which varies depending on strategy.

This game is another case of a game that is good depending on how much time you put into it. More specifically, if you have time to dump into learning the many strategies and mastering the economy, this game is absolutely incredible with insane depth. However, if you don't have that time then I woudln't exactly reccomend it since there are so many complex moving parts within the game that take a lot of time to grasp. Since the game is a bit older, I can't really reccomned this game since the playerbase is somewhat dwindling, however, since it's free you can go ahead and try it if it sounds like something that would interest you.

Cuphead:

Definitely the most unique game on this list, Cuphead is a extremely stylized run-and-gun video game developed by Studio MDHR. The game is deceptively difficult with a very simple combat system, with unique bosses in vainn of the Contra video game series. This game is extremely unqiue with everything being hand drawn and animated in an old timey, rubber hose style that makes the game almost immediately recognizable.

This game is another extremely easy reccomend from me due to its highly addictive gameplay and extremely charming art style, this game is a masterpiece. The only thing that bumps it down from being a perfect game is that some of the bosses may be a little too difficult for a more casual audience, however to me the art style and gameplay make the game far too enjoyable to pass up on.
